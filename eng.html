What is PET/CT

PET/CT is a hybrid method of radiological and nuclear diagnostics performed using radiopharmaceuticals. This method allows the detection of oncological changes at very early stages before they become visible on CT (Computed Tomography) or MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)

PET/CT is an irreplaceable method for the diagnosis of oncological diseases. It not only allows detecting cancer at early stages but also helps assess its spread, plan treatment, and monitor its effectiveness. In addition, it can reveal tumors that remain invisible even with CT or MRI scans.

The digital-generation PET/CT ensures maximum sensitivity and accuracy. The device is also equipped with artificial intelligence (AI) programs, which reduce the amount of radioactive material needed for the patient while still providing high-quality images.

This technology makes it possible to detect tumor lesions as small as 3 mm, which greatly contributes to early and effective treatment of diseases.

When is PET/CT Used

• Detection and diagnosis of cancer (lung, breast, skin, brain, lymphatic system, etc.)

• Staging of cancer and evaluation of metastases

• Monitoring the response to cancer treatment (e.g., chemotherapy or radiotherapy)

• In some cases, detection of infections or inflammations

PET/CT Scan Procedure

1. The entire procedure takes about 3 hours, including preparation and scanning.

2. After injection of FDG, the patient must remain at rest, either sitting or lying down.

3. The scan itself is performed while lying down with arms folded above the head and lasts approximately 20 minutes.

PET/CT Scan Preparation

• Avoid physical exertion the day before the test.

• Wear comfortable clothing without metal parts.

• Eat protein-rich foods and limit foods containing carbohydrates.

• Bring your passport, social security card, medical summaries, and discs of previous tests.

• At least 6 hours before the test, do not consume food, coffee, tea, caffeine-containing liquids, or sweet juices (only plain water is allowed).

• Inform your doctor of any chronic illnesses, allergies, or pregnancy. If breastfeeding, consult your doctor in advance to determine the safe period after the test for resuming breastfeeding.

Post-PET/CT Instructions

• Drink several glasses of water to help eliminate the radioactive substance from the body
